How to roll out QR labels

1. Catalog the items first. Each item needs a record (name, photo, location) before its label means anything.

2. Print and attach. Generate labels right from the platform and stick them on bins, racks, and high-value items.

3. Use durable labels. Polypropylene weatherproof labels for bins and equipment; iron-on or sew-in for costumes so they survive the wash.

4. Make scanning the habit. Scan on checkout and return, and your records stay current with almost no extra effort.

Frequently asked questions

Do students need an app to scan?

No — any modern phone camera reads the label and opens the record in a browser. Nothing to download.

What labels should I print on?

Weatherproof polypropylene for bins and equipment; iron-on or sew-in labels for costumes. Plain paper falls off and gets water-damaged.

Is QR labeling included for free?

Yes — QR labels and photos are included on the free plan (up to 25 items). Pro ($15/month) adds unlimited items, reports, and the Backstage Exchange.
